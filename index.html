Agentic AI Architecture

The Bitter Lesson is about to hit agentic AI builders

Hand-crafting agent architectures — stitching system prompts, tool definitions, routing logic, guardrails — is the same pattern the bitter lesson has punished every time. A new open-source framework from UC Berkeley (optimize_anything / GEPA) treats any text artifact as an optimizable parameter. A 10-line agent stub automatically evolved into a 300+ line multi-stage system. ARC-AGI accuracy went from 32.5% to 89.5%. No human designed that architecture.

The craft of building AI applications is shifting — from designing agents to defining outcomes.

Agentic AI Benchmarking & Adoption

Visualising Agentic AI as a Triangle — not a straight line

Two of the most-cited studies pull in opposite directions: METR's "agents are doubling in capability" and MIT's "95% of GenAI projects fail." Looking at only one leaves you either overestimating what you can deploy, or underestimating what's possible. The missing lens is the tasks corner — GDP-Eval-style evidence showing how frontier models already perform on the work we actually pay people to do.

Capabilities tell you what's possible. Tasks tell you where value appears first. Adoption tells you how hard it is to ship.

AI Safety & Assurance · IMDA · Jan 2026

Making AI Assurance Work in the Real World

With Dr Martin Saerbeck, I demonstrated how to test a multi-turn conversational AI career guidance chatbot — the kind of system where small errors compound across turns and influence high-stakes decisions. Our objectives: discover and characterise risks, define testable assurance requirements aligned to IMDA's Starter Kit three pillars (Hallucination, Undesirable Content, Adversarial Vulnerability), and generate reusable artefacts others can apply.

GenAI systems are probabilistic. They can be helpful and still be wrong, unsafe, or manipulable. Testing is not a checkbox — it is how we earn trust.

Multimodal AI · A*STAR · SWITCH 2025

The Power of Possibilities with MERaLiON

Panellist at "Imagine AI – The Power of Possibilities with MERaLiON" at SWITCH 2025. MERaLiON is Southeast Asia's empathetic multimodal LLM, designed for the region's diverse languages, cultures, and communication styles. We explored how multimodal LLMs reduce solution complexity, cut response latency, and how hyper-localising AI is the foundation for meaningful adoption and trust — not a side concern.

At NCS, we evaluate MERaLiON not just in isolation but as part of a larger AI stack — integrating with internal workflows, tuning for performance, measuring deployment cost and resilience.

AI at Work · Masterclass · 2026

AI is changing tasks before it changes jobs

Most companies are investing in the wrong place. The gap between adoption and impact is the biggest risk. In his Budget 2026 speech, PM Wong announced a National AI Council he'll personally chair and named the real challenge: many firms say they are using AI, but end-to-end transformation requires organising data, rebuilding systems, redesigning processes and jobs, and retraining workers.

That gap — between adoption and impact — is what I addressed in my AI at Work masterclass. 200+ use cases surfaced through NCS Group's SunshineAI hackathons, and a knowledge-sharing culture that turns individual discoveries into organisational learning.
